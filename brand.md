# Brand

## Voice

How the product sounds in UI copy, headings, CTAs, and error messages.

- Short sentences. Direct. No jargon.
- Data-first framing — show the number before the interpretation.
- Understated confidence. No hype words.
- Never use: "powerful", "seamless", "revolutionary", "unlock", "supercharge"

---

## The User

A solo founder or analyst who reads more than they act. They spot market shifts early but lose the insight before they can act on it. They want a system that captures the signal and forces them to commit to a thesis.

---

## Visual Rules

- Dark mode only. No toggle.
- Terminal aesthetic — monospace secondary font, tight spacing, data density over whitespace.
- Green as the signal color — confirms, validates, indicates go.
- Muted grays for inactive state. Nothing decorative.

## Emotional Arc

- Land: "This is for someone who pays attention."
- Read: "I can see exactly where to put each signal."
- Use: "This makes my thinking visible."
- Return: "I want to check if my thesis held."

---

## Copy Examples

- Hero: "Stop losing the signal in the noise."
- CTA: "Start capturing signal"
- Weekly digest: "What you spotted. What it means. What you'd bet on."
- Empty state: "Nothing here yet. Drop a signal."
- Error: "Something went wrong. Try again."
